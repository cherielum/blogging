Chocolate Chip Cookies

  • Time: 1 hour 5 minutes
  • Serves: 15 people or less!
  • Level: Easy
Quantity Ingredients
1/2 cup unsalted butter
3/4 cup packed dark brown sugar
3/4 cup sugar
2 whole large eggs
1 tsp pure vanill extract
12 oz bag of semi-sweet chocolate chips
2-1/4 cup all purpose flour
3/4 tsp baking soda
1 tsp fine salt

Step 1: Evenly position 2 racks in the middle of the oven and preheat to 375 degrees F. (on convection setting if you have it.) Line 2 baking sheets with parchment paper or silicone sheets. (If you only have 1 baking sheet, let it cool completely between batches.)

Step 2: Put the butter in a microwave safe bowl, cover and microwave on medium power until melted. (Alternatively melt in a small saucepan.) Cool slightly. Whisk the sugars, eggs, butter and vanilla in a large bowl until smooth.

Step 3: Whisk the flour, baking soda and salt in another bowl. Stir the dry ingredients into the wet ingredients with a wooden spoon; take care not to over mix. Stir in the chocolate chips or chunks.

Step 4: Scoop heaping tablespoons of the dough onto the prepared pans. Wet hands slightly and roll the dough into balls. Space the cookies about 2-inches apart on the pans. Bake, until golden, but still soft in the center, 12 to 16 minutes, depending on how chewy or crunchy you like your cookies. Transfer hot cookies with a spatula to a rack to cool. Serve.

Extra tip: Store cookies in a tightly sealed container for up to 5 days.

Frozen Hot Chocolate

  • Time: 10 minutes
  • Serves: 1-2 small mouths
  • Level: Easy
Quantity Ingredients
1 cup ice
3 tbsp hot chocolate powder
1/4 tsp vanilla
1/3 cup almond milk or low fat milk

Step 1: Put all ingredients into the blender, blend until smooth.

Step 2: Enjoy!

Extra tip: For a smoother and creamier texture blend in ice cream. This will also prevent the drink from settling quickly.

Extra tip x2: For a chocolate lover, add chocolate syrup to top it off

1 tsp salt
2 whole eggs
1 cup milk
1 cup milk
